Introduction

Mr. McBay is the principal of K. R. McBay Company. K. R. McBay Company is a firm that appraises and consults on a wide range of property types for government agencies involved in eminent domain, for collateral valuation for lenders, and for property owners and investors. Property types appraised include agricultural properties such as orchards, vineyards, open cropland, agribusiness facilities such as grain elevators and fertilizer plants. Commercial properties include industrial, office, retail, multi-family, land, and subdivision properties. Specialty areas include conservation easement valuation and valuations for eminent domain purposes.

Mr. McBay has over 28 years valuation experience. After graduation from college and service as an infantry officer in the U.S. Marine Corps, he began his real estate career with a property management and acquisition company providing acquisition and management analysis and valuation; then working with a major life insurance company as a real estate investment analyst, and for a major bank providing management and property valuation for acquired properties.

Mr. McBay began working as a fee appraiser in 1991 for appraisal firms in the Sacramento area, and began his own firm, K. R. McBay Company, in 2000. His extensive experience and professional training has equipped him to deal with a wide range of complex appraisal assignments. Geographically the firm appraises in an extensive area from Fresno County in the south to Butte and Yolo Counties in the north.

    Introduction

    Mr. Souza is a senior appraiser with K .R. McBay Appraisal Company, a real estate appraisal firm that engages in a wide variety of real estate valuation and consultation assignments. In 2007 Mr. Souza began his career in real estate as an associate appraiser with K. R. McBay Appraisal Company. Mr. Souza has gained experience in the valuation of a wide range of commercial and agricultural properties. Specific property types have included: office, retail, industrial, multi-family residential, rural residential, commercial and residential land; as well as mixed-use and special-purpose properties. Special-purpose properties have included religious facilities, walnut huller/dryer facilities, aqua-culture facilities, dairy facilities and RV/mini storage facilities. Agricultural properties have included orchards, vineyards, open cropland, pastureland, rangeland, agricultural processing facilities, including cold storage facilities. Mr. Souza has also gained considerable experience in the valuation of a variety of properties affected by eminent domain.
